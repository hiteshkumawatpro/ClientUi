INDUSTRY - MANAGEMENT CONSULTING - ONE OF THE BIG 10
THE CONTEXT

New joinees (our participants) were coming on
board with pay packages almost double the
market standard.
They had been recruited through a rigorous
process that emphasized their ability to process
lots of information quickly and formulate
solutions based on rock-solid analysis.

THE PROBLEM

The organization got it right when it came to getting these people on board for their analytical ability. But closing a deal also takes lots of practice and experience. The new joinees didn't have that kind of experience  but were being compared to people with 2 or 3x their experience. And this comparison was made explicit - the organization would publish the number of deals cracked / new projects won from the existing client by each individual (new joinees included) on a leaderboard. And so, the game wasn't only about putting in long hours or about meeting targets. It was also about staying ahead on the leaderboard. Put another way - huge performance pressure which people were finding very difficult to cope with.

SURVIVAL

But these were highly motivated people we were dealing with, who wanted to do well and were motivated to learn
any skill that would get them slotted higher up on the leaderboard.

STEP 1

We began with a question - were these people not closing any deals. Nope. They could close deals with a select type of clients - if the client could see patterns, understood how systems worked or were analytically oriented - no problem. But if they were dealing with someone who was more intuitive in nature and had their gut play a large role in their decisions - the deal would go south.

STEP 2

We designed a significant part of the program around this
understanding and began with sensitizing them to the different
communication stules that people had and the need to flex their own communication style.
You could tell the best story in the world, but if the listeners didn't see it as relevant you'd rapidly lose their attention: "the story belongs to the listener...."

 We worked with the participants to identify differences in the
communication stules the had encountered with their clients. Here
are the top 4 Task oriented vs. people oriented Detailed oriented vs. I- require-the-big-picture-first Wanting to be given direction vs. sharing the options and allowing the person to define the way forward Risk averse and cautious vs. speed of execution.

Keeping these differences in mind we got them to structure their
narratives, for example if the client was risk averse then the
participants would need to upfront the risks in their narrative and
demonstrate how they planned to obviate them.

THE OUTCOME

We not only got good feedback but participants were able to identify
exactly how they would change their communication styles and how it would benefit them. They were on their way to closing more projects.
